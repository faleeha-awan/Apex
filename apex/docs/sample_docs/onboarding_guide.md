# New member onboarding guide

Welcome to the team. This document covers everything you need to know in your first two weeks. Read it fully before your first workshop day.

## Workshop access and hours

The workshop is located in the faculty of Mechanical Engineering, building 34, ground floor, room 0.34. Your student card will be activated for 24/7 access within three working days of joining. Until then, always come with an existing member. Do not prop the door open or share your access with anyone outside the team.

Workshop hours are flexible — most people work between 09:00 and 22:00. Late-night sessions are common before competition deadlines. Always log your hours in the shared spreadsheet on Google Drive under "Team Operations / Hour Tracking." This is mandatory for insurance reasons.

## Communication

We use Slack for all team communication. Join the workspace using your university email at forze-delft.slack.com. The key channels are:

- **#general** — announcements from the team lead
- **#embedded** — software and electronics discussions
- **#simcon** — simulation and control team
- **#fuel-cell** — powertrain and fuel cell team
- **#chassis** — mechanical and aerodynamics
- **#random** — non-work chat

Response time expectation: reply to direct messages within 24 hours on weekdays. You are not expected to be available on weekends unless we are near a competition deadline.

## Documentation

All design decisions must be documented in Confluence. This is non-negotiable. Every year we lose weeks of work because a graduating member took critical knowledge with them. When you make a decision, write it down: what you decided, why you decided it, and what alternatives you rejected. Future members will thank you.

Project files live in Google Drive. The folder structure is: Team [Year] / [Department] / [Subsystem]. Never save working files locally only. Use the naming convention: YYYY-MM-DD_description_version.

## Getting started checklist

1. Get workshop card access activated
2. Join Slack and introduce yourself in #general
3. Request Confluence and Google Drive access from the information manager
4. Read the safety protocol document before your first hands-on session
5. Schedule a 1-on-1 with your department lead in your first week
6. Complete the mandatory electrical safety training (online, takes 45 minutes)
7. Shadow an existing member on a real task before working independently

## Team culture

We are a high-performance team that also cares deeply about each other. Work hard but know your limits. If you are struggling with your university coursework because of team commitments, talk to your department lead — we will find a solution. Nobody's degree should suffer because of Forze.

We celebrate successes together and debrief failures without blame. When something breaks or a mistake is made, the first question is "how do we prevent this next time," not "whose fault was this."
